## What is Interference?

Imagine throwing a paper plane from one hill of a mountain to another.  
- If a strong wind is blowing upward, it helps the plane fly from one hill to the other, and with time even higher and farther. This way your paper plane can reach hills that are even further up. 
- But if the wind pushes your plane downward into the valley, the plane gets stuck and never comes out.  

This is what **interference** is like: sometimes waves, just like a mountain, add together to make something **stronger**, and sometimes they **cancel each other** out. 

You can also see interference with water waves.  
- When two waves meet at the same time, their ripples can join together to make a **bigger wave** (constructive interference).  
- Or they can crash into each other so that the water goes almost **flat** (destructive interference), so we can't see any movement anymore.  

![Wave Interference](/posts/assets/entries/interference/waves_interference.png)

So waves, just like mountains, also have "hills" and "valleys". If hills of two waves meet, they produce an even stroger wave. Just like when your paper plane jumps from one hill to another hill, the wind can support it to reach even higher hills.
But if the "valley" of one wave meets the "hill" of another wave, the wave dissapears. Similar to if your paper plane moves from a hill to a valley of a mountain, your plane is stuck and can't continue flying. 

This happens with all kinds of waves — water, sound, light, even radio signals!

## Interference in Quantum Worlds
In our world, light can act a bit like water waves. But long ago, scientists were unsure and argued, whether light made of little balls, or if it is a wave?
To test this, they shined light through two tiny doors. If light were just little balls, you would only see two bright spots on the wall behind the doors.
But something surprising happened! The light made a pattern of bright and dark stripes — just like ripples on water when waves overlap. That’s called interference, and it showed that light behaves like a wave.

---

## Why It’s Important

Interference is not just fun to watch in water — it’s also what makes many modern things work, like headphones that cancel noise, and even **quantum computers**. Quantum computers use interference like a clever trick: they cancel out wrong answers and boost the right ones, just like a friendly wind helping your paper plane reach the top of the next hill.
